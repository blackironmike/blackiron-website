Nutrition FAQs

Common questions about TDEE, macros, and nutrition coaching

What is TDEE?

TDEE stands for Total Daily Energy Expenditure. It's the total number of calories your body burns in a day, including your basal metabolic rate (BMR), physical activity, and the energy used to digest food. Knowing your TDEE is essential for setting calorie targets.

What are macros?

Macros — short for macronutrients — are the three main nutrients your body needs in large amounts: protein, carbohydrates, and fat. Each plays a different role in performance, recovery, and body composition. Tracking macros helps you eat the right amount of each.

How accurate is this calculator?

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, one of the most validated formulas for estimating metabolic rate. It's a great starting point, but individual factors like genetics, muscle mass, and metabolic adaptation can affect your actual needs. For precise numbers, an InBody scan provides exact body composition data.

Should I track macros or just calories?

Calories determine whether you gain or lose weight, but macros determine what you gain or lose. Tracking macros ensures you're getting enough protein to build and preserve muscle, enough carbs to fuel your training, and enough fat for hormonal health.

How do I know my exact body composition?

We use the InBody 270 body composition scanner at Black Iron Athletics. It measures your lean muscle mass, body fat percentage, water balance, and more in about 60 seconds. Every consultation includes a free InBody scan so we can build your plan on real data — not guesswork.

Yes. We offer personalized nutrition coaching that includes an InBody body composition scan, custom macro targets, meal planning guidance, and ongoing check-ins. Book a free consultation to learn more about how we can help you reach your goals faster.
